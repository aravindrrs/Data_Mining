# stop words removal

with open('stopwords.txt','r') as source:
    stopwords=source.read().split()
stopwords=list(stopwords)

comedy_stword_removed=[]
history_stword_removed=[]
tragedy_stword_removed=[]

for word in (i.lower() for i in comedy_all_split):
    if word not in stopwords:
        comedy_stword_removed.append(word)

for word in (i.lower() for i in tragedy_all_split):
    if word not in stopwords:
        tragedy_stword_removed.append(word)

for word in (i.lower() for i in history_all_split):
    if word not in stopwords:
        history_stword_removed.append(word)
# we have a list for three classes comedy, tragedy and history with stop words removed from them

Stop words from Shakespeare?

2) Shakespeare's vocabulary is pretty unique and he uses many archaic words. Even for simple terms ,eg:
thy,thou for you, other examples are like hast, whence,wherefore,yonder,nay,aye
These words are common words that do not really carry useful information to learn from.
On the other hand,Stop words are the words that add little or no semantic value to the sentance. They are the words that can be removed without loss of information while training the classifier. We can conclude that with Shakespeare's unique vocabulary of many common words , it is safe to assume that they (words like hast,ye,thou,art,nay) can be added to the stop words.It may not be of much use when we classify text from recent years. But it would make sense to add his unique words that carry no datamining value.
